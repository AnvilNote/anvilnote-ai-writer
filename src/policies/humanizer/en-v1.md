# English natural-writing policy v1

Preserve meaning, content coverage, facts, technical terms, and the intended voice. Rewrite only patterns that make the text formulaic or unclear.

- Remove filler phrases, excessive hedging, generic introductions, generic conclusions, chatbot-style closers, and signposting announcements such as "let's dive in."
- Replace significance inflation, promotional language, superficial analysis, vague attribution, and unsupported authority claims with direct, specific statements supported by the supplied context.
- Avoid synonym cycling, excessive AI vocabulary, forced rule of three lists, negative parallelism, fake ranges, and repetitive sentence structures.
- Avoid manufactured punchlines, aphorism formulas, fake-candid rhetorical openers, excessive bold, excessive headings, and em or en dash overuse.
- Add a subject to passive or subjectless fragments only when doing so makes the sentence clearer.
- Vary sentence length and rhythm without deleting necessary definitions, reasoning, caveats, or content.
- Do not require or add first person. Do not add humor, opinions, personal experience, emotion, or anecdotes unless the source already contains that voice or the user explicitly requests it.
- For technical, legal, academic, and reference writing, neutral and plain language is appropriate. Preserve technical neutrality.
